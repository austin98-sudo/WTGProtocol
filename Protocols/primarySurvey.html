1. Form general impression of the patient. Consider apperance, work of breathing, and circulation to skin. If a life-threatening condition is found, treat it immediately. · · · · · ·
2. Assess patient's mental status (maintain spinal immobilization if needed). · · · · · ·
a. Alert | Responds to Verbal stimuli | Responds to Painful stimuli | Unresponsive to verbal/painful stimuli (no gag or cough). · · · · · ·
b. If the victim is unresponsive with no breathing or no normal breathing (i.e. only gasping), see CARDIAC ARREST - ADULT or CARDIAC ARREST - PEDIATRIC. · · · · · ·
3. Address any MASSIVE HEMORRHAGE · · · · · ·
a. Locate the source of any major bleeding · · · · · ·
b. Provide direct pressure until a clot forms, perferably with hemostatic gauze in a wound packing technique · · · · · ·
c. If necessary, utilize commercial tourniquets to control bleeding, DO NOT DELAY TOURNIQUET APPLICATION - IF IN DOUBT, APPLY TOURNIQUET · · · · · ·
