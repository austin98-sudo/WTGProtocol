Generic Name: Albuterol (al-byoo'ter-ole)
Trade Name: Airet®, Proventil®, Repetabs®, Respirol®, Ventiolin®, Volmax®, Combivent® (combinged wth ipratropium)
Chemical Class: Sympathomimetic amine; β2-adrenergic agonist
Therapeutic Class: Antiasthmatic bronchodilator
Action: Albuterol is a selective β2-adrenergic agonist with a minimal number of side effects. It causes prompt bronchodilation and has a duration of action of approximately 5 hours
Pharmacokinetics: Onset: 5-15 minutes. Peak 1-1 ½ hours. Duration 4-6 hours. T½ = 2 ½ to 4 hours
Indications:
  • Bronchial asthma
  • Reversible bronchospam associated with chronic bronchitis and emphysema
  • Anaphylactic respiratory distress
  • Crush Syndrome [INT, PM, APM] [Medical Control]
Contraindications:
  • Hypersensitivty to the drug
Precautions
Pregnancy Cat. C
  • Hypertension (SBP greater than 180)
  • Tachycardia (HR greater than 140 adult, HR greater than 180 child)
  • Severe cardiac disease
  • Hyperthyroidism
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Convulsive disorders
Side Effects:
  • CNS: dizziness, headache, stimulation, tremors
  • CV: chest pain ,dysrhythmias, hypertension, palpitations, tachycardia
  • GI: nausea, vomiting
Administration:
  • Using a small volume nebulizer, adjust the oxygen flowmeter to 6 to 10 L/min to produce a steady, visible mist.
  • Adult: Give 2.5 gm (3 mL of 0.083% solution) with a mouthpiece or facemask. Repeat every 10 minutes, up to 4 treatments, if needed.
  • Pediatric: Give 2.5 mg (3 mL of 0.083% solution) with a mouthpiece or blow-by. Repeat every 10 minute, up to 4 treatments, if needed.
Supply: Unit dose vials containing 2.5 mg in 3 mL
Notes:
  • The possibility of developing unpleasant side effects increases when albuterol is administered with other sympathetic agonists
  • β-blockers may blunt the pharmacological effects of albuterol.
  • Albuterol is also supplied in metered-dose inhalers (MDI) that deliver 90 mcg per inhalation. Be sure to obtain a complete medication history detaling administration times and frequency of use of home inhalation therapy. Overdoses of inhalers cause bronchoconstriction and possibly death
