2. Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation · · · · · ·
3. Assess for signs of trauma. Provide spinal motion restriction as necessary. · · · · · ·
4. Administer oxygen, if necessary · · · · · ·
5. Establish an INT or IV of LACTATED RINGERS or NORMAL SALINE at KVO · · · ·
6. Perform rapid glucose determination · · · · · ·
a. For glucose less tahn 60 mg/dL, refer to the HYPOGLYCEMIA protocol. · · · · ·
b. For glucose greater than 300 mg/dL, refer to the HYPERGLYCEMIA protocol. · · · · ·
7. For suspected overdose, refer to the appropriate TOXICOLOGICAL EMERGENCIES protocol. · · · · · ·
8. Obtain a 12-LEAD ECG. · · · · · ·
9. Transport as soon as possible · · · · ·

Key Points: ALTERED MENTAL STATUS

  • Possible causes of unconsciousness or altered mental status (AEIOU-TIPS)
    • A - Acidosis, alcohol
    • E - Epilepsy
    • I - Infection
    • O - Overdose
    • U - Uremia (kidney failure)
    • T - Trauma, tumor
    • I - Insulin
    • P - Psychosis
    • S - Stroke
